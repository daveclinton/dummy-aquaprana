Feeding Best Practices for Shrimp Aquaculture

R
Dr. R. Kumar
Published Jan 18, 2026 Feeding Pond Mgmt
A comprehensive guide to optimising feeding schedules, feed selection, and feeding frequency for L. vannamei in intensive pond systems. Covers FCR improvement, automatic feeders, and waste reduction.

Optimal feeding is the single biggest driver of profitable shrimp aquaculture. Over-feeding leads to water quality degradation, disease, and wasted cost. Under-feeding slows growth and extends the crop cycle. Getting the balance right requires understanding your stocking density, water temperature, and shrimp behaviour.

1. Establishing a Baseline Feed Rate

Begin with manufacturer recommended feed rates — typically 3–5% of estimated biomass per day for juvenile shrimp, tapering to 2–3% as shrimp grow. Adjust weekly based on body weight checks via cast net sampling.

2. Feed Tray Monitoring

Place 4–6 feed trays per hectare at 60cm depth. Check after 90 minutes. If trays are empty, increase feed by 5%. If leftover feed exceeds 20%, reduce by 5%. This simple feedback loop prevents both over- and under-feeding.

3. Feed Timing & Frequency

Distribute feed 4–5 times daily, avoiding peak heat (12–2pm). Dawn and dusk feeds are critical as shrimp are most active. Automated feeders can improve consistency and reduce labour costs by 40%.
